What is a French Press really?

We can’t enjoy ground coffee without filtering the coffee grounds first. French press solves this problem by filtering the ground coffee by separating the coffee grounds from the brewed coffee with its filter. How each part works: Plunger helps us push and pull the filtering system. Lid prevents the coffee from getting cold. Spiral plate, mesh filter and cross plate filters the coffee grounds. Carafe is where the brewed coffee is stored and the handle helps us pour the coffee from the french press to the cup. .

Let's ask some questions, shall we?

I decided to interview my roommates and see what they think about french presses.

How do you determine coffee to water ratio?

Nisha: I use 2 spoons of coffee and fill the water halfway, which makes 2 cups of coffee.

Asli:I fill the bottom of a mug with coffee grounds and then add water until the top.

Safiya: I use 2 spoons of coffee and fill the water 3/4. That makes a cup of coffee..

When do you stop the plunger, do you instantly push it all the way down?

Nisha: I wait 5 minutes before pushing the plunger down.

Asli:I wait couple of minutes before pushing it down.

Safiya: I wait as long as the coffee package indicates to wait for the coffee to brew and then I push the plunger down.

How do you press the plunger?

Nisha, Safiya: I push it slowly.

Asli: I push it fast.

At what temperature do you pour the water over the coffee grounds?

Nisha: Boiling hot.

Asli: Hot.

Safiya: I boil my kettle and then wait a few seconds before I pour the water into the french press.

How long does it take you to prepare coffee with a french press?

Nisha: It takes me about 6-7 minutes, very minimal effort.

Asli: It depends on whether I am cleaning it after. It might take 5 to 6 minutes.

Safiya: Approximately 4 minutes excluding the time it takes for the water to boil.

Why do you prefer using french press to make coffee over other appliances?

Nisha: I think that it tastes a lot better. I sometimes like to put cinnamon in with the coffee grounds to make it tastier. .

Asli: It tastes good and has a stronger kick.

Safiya: The coffee taste is better and you get a whole pot of coffee.

Is there anything you find particularly inconvenient about french presses, (ex: hard to wash after usage)?

Nisha: No, cleaning is super easy, especially if you rinse it right after making the coffee. I think the only thing is that you can’t understand the interface without written instructions.

Asli: Cleaning the french press is diffucult, I wish that it cleaned itself.

Safiya: Yes, it is hard to wash afterwards because you need to separate all the parts to clean it properly. The wire nets can also start to fray at the ends. Also pulling the plunger up after it is down is impossible.

Observations

Nisha: She waited water to boil and then she poured the water to the french press. She removed the lid by pulling the plunger. She measured the amount of coffee grounds with a tablespoon. She then inserted the lid to avoid heat to escape. She timed the brewing process with a watch. She put back the lid and pushed the plunger slowly until the filter touched the bottom of the flask.

Asli: She didn’t wait water to boil before she poured the water to the french press. She also didn’t measure the amount of coffee grounds she used. In addition, she didn’t time the brewing process and pushed the plunge when she thought the coffee was ready without using any timers.

Safiya: She waited for water to boil then let the water rest for a minute to ensure that the water was not boiling hot. She then removed the removed the lid by pulling the plunger. She measured the amount of coffee ground with a tablespoon. She poured the water before putting the coffee grounds. Then she pushed the filter by pushing the plunger until the filter touched the coffee-water mixture. She then timed 4 minutes with her phone and pushed the plunger slowly until the filter touched the bottom of the flask.
